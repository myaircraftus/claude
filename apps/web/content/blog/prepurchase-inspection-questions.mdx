# 27 Questions to Ask Before Buying a Used Aircraft

The pre-purchase inspection is only half of your due diligence. The paper records are the other half — and they reveal problems the airframe alone never will.

## Airframe history

1. Is there a continuous maintenance record from new?
2. Have any logbooks been replaced or recreated?
3. Is there evidence of damage history?
4. Has the aircraft ever been de-registered?
5. How many owners has it had?

## Compliance

6. Is every applicable AD documented with a compliance entry?
7. Are recurring ADs tracked to the next-due interval?
8. Is the ELT battery replacement current?
9. Are the transponder and pitot/static checks current?
10. Is the 100-hour (if commercial use) or annual current?

## Engine and propeller

11. How many hours since the last major overhaul?
12. Who performed the overhaul — factory, field, or owner-rebuilt?
13. Is the overhaul logbook included?
14. Has the engine made TBO in its history?
15. What is the service bulletin compliance status?

## Equipment

16. Is the equipment list current?
17. Are all installed avionics on the list?
18. Is there an approved weight & balance, dated within the last year?
19. Are 337s present for every major alteration?

## Damage and corrosion

20. Any logged damage history?
21. Any evidence of undisclosed damage?
22. Has the aircraft been hangared?
23. Is there a corrosion inspection on file?

## The sale itself

24. Is there a clear title?
25. Are there outstanding liens?
26. Is the bill of sale in the seller's legal name?
27. Has the FAA Registry been checked within the last week?
